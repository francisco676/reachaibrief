section 05

top-performing format.

across the 14 LinkedIn posts we analyzed, one format outperforms everything else by an order of magnitude: lead-magnet giveaway with comment-trigger CTA.

the Looker Studio dashboard post, "my team didn't want me to give this away for free," earned 857 reactions · 3,572 comments · 12 reposts that's roughly 30x the comment volume of the next-best non-lead-magnet post.

the pattern: friction-with-team hook → 3-tab asset description → behind-the-build framing → "comment 'AEO' and i'll send it over" CTA. the friction-with-team line is the trust device. readers infer that if his own team didn't want this shared, it must be valuable.

two more lead-magnet posts confirm the pattern: the AI-replaces-80%-of-GTM-playbooks workshop post with Mica from Amplemarket (108 reactions, 222 comments) and the FAQ guide post (52 reactions, 85 comments). all three follow the same structural template.

the format ranking we'd over-index on

  1. 01

    mode F · lead-magnet giveaway with comment-trigger CTA

    the Reach team has multiple potential lead magnets to deploy. each one is a content event. this is the priority format.

  2. 02

    mode D · founder narrative

    the $500K-ARR-by-stage post earned 176 reactions and 46 comments, the strongest non-lead-magnet performer. pivot stories live here.

  3. 03

    mode A · tactical playbook

    the website-conversion 9-tip post and similar. builds long-tail authority and gets bookmarked.

the vulnerable founder mode appears once in 14 posts (the $250K-in-90-days post: "honestly? i'm happy… but it also feels weirdly small", 176 reactions). we'd use this register sparingly, roughly 1 per 8-10 confident posts. it's the foil that makes the bullish playbook posts more credible by contrast.

the lowest-engagement format in the corpus was a pure hiring post (36 reactions). we wouldn't lead with that format unless paired with a milestone narrative. when the $1M-ARR celebration paired hiring with milestone, it earned 82 reactions and 32 comments.

section 07

how we sound when we write as José.

sentence rhythm

average paragraph length 1.6 sentences. whitespace-heavy. short directive kickers ("that changes now." / "make it count." / "LFG ⚡"). mix of long compound-clause thesis sentences with arrow-bulleted enumeration.

bullet discipline

multiple bullet styles per post, content-driven not signature:

→ outcome / accomplishment lists
↳ sub-points / explanatory clauses
✦ tactical bullets within bold sections
* case-study results
1. 2. 3. sequential tips
unicode bold for section headers

vocabulary we lean into

compound real actually obsessing heads-down shipping bootstrapped hair on fire vibe-code AI slop non-deterministic scientific, experimental mindset category leader LFG ⚡

vocabulary we avoid

thrilled excited to announce humbled rocketship 10x'd disrupting revolutionizing paradigm shift cutting-edge state-of-the-art

José's voice is anti-hype. we protect that.

closing patterns

→ punchy directive ("make it count.")
→ comment-trigger CTA ("drop names in the comments.")
→ big-thesis kicker ("figuring that out is the new founder-market fit.")
→ personal sign-off ("LFG ⚡")
→ question close ("what's it like for you?")
→ synthesis line ("match your channels to where you are, not where you want to be.")

section 08

the AEO market context.

category state

AEO (Answer Engine Optimization, sometimes GEO or AISO) is the emerging discipline of being recommended inside ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews. Reach AI is one of the early operators-with-platform players in a category that has roughly 12 months of vocabulary and no playbook.

competitors

Profound (early AEO leader, raised funding), Athena, Otterly.AI, Goodie, Daydream, Peec.ai. plus traditional SEO tools racing in: Ahrefs Brand Radar, Semrush AI Toolkit.

buyer reality

94% of B2B buyers now use AI during their purchasing process (per Maja Voje's research José contributed to). most companies have no idea what AI says about them, or worse, AI says nothing. buyers are shortlisting brands inside ChatGPT and Perplexity before clicking a blue link.

timing dynamic

the category is at the moment of peak arbitrage. buyers are using AI search, but very few companies are winning from it yet. Reach's positioning (operator-grade, hybrid platform + senior expert) is built for that gap.
